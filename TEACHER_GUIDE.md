### Advancing the Timeline

Each turn follows this pattern:

1. **Click "Advance Timeline"**
   - Historical event displays on screen
   - Example: "4500 BCE: Agricultural Revolution"
   
2. **Read Event Aloud** (recommended)
   - Explain historical context
   - Point out affected regions
   - Discuss implications

3. **Growth Phase Happens Automatically**
   - All civilizations grow (houses, population)
   - Stats recalculate based on population
   - Students see updated stats instantly

4. **Action Phase (2-5 minutes)**
   - Students can now take actions:
     - Build structures (temples, walls, etc.)
     - Declare wars (after 670 BCE)
     - Form alliances (requires Diplomacy)
     - Found religions (after 1000 BCE)
   - Monitor actions in real-time on dashboard

5. **Repeat**: Advance â†’ Explain â†’ Actions â†’ Repeat

### Special Events

#### Wonder Assignment (Years: 1300 BCE, 44 CE)
```
When prompted:
1. View top civilizations by Industry
2. Click civilization name to assign wonder
3. Effects apply immediately
   - Great Wall: +Defense
   - Pyramids: +Industry
   - Colossus: +Diplomacy
   etc.
```

#### War Declarations (After 670 BCE)
```
When students declare war:
1. Combat resolves automatically
2. Attacker Martial vs Defender (Martial + Defense)
3. Loser is conquered (eliminated from game)
4. Conquered students reassigned to winner's civ
5. Special message: "The chair has claimed another victim..."
```

#### Religion Founding (After 1000 BCE)
```
Students can found religions:
1. Name their religion
2. Choose 2-3 tenants (beliefs)
   - Monotheism, Polytheism, Divine Right, etc.
3. +5 Faith bonus
4. Can spread to allied civilizations
```

#### Overview Panel
- **Civilizations Tab**: See all stats sorted by population
- **Alliances Tab**: Visual network of who's allied
- **Wars Tab**: Combat history and results
- **Events Tab**: Log of all timeline events

### Learning Objectives Alignment
- **Geography**: Identify ancient regions and their characteristics
- **History**: Sequence major events from 50,000 BCE to 362 CE
- **Cause & Effect**: Understand how events affected civilizations
- **Decision-Making**: Strategic resource allocation
- **Collaboration**: Alliance formation and diplomacy

### Discussion Questions (By Era)
**Paleolithic (50,000-8,500 BCE)**
- Why did early humans migrate slowly?
- What limited population growth?

**Agricultural Revolution (4,500 BCE)**
- Why did Egypt and Mesopotamia advance faster?
- What role did rivers play in civilization?

**Bronze Age (2,250-1,200 BCE)**
- Why was writing important for development?
- How did trade networks spread disease?

**Iron Age (1,000-670 BCE)**
- Why did iron replace bronze?
- What role did religion play in society?

**Classical Period (480-301 BCE)**
- Why could cities support more population?
- What made Greece and Persia powerful?

**Roman Era (270 BCE-362 CE)**
- Why did Rome dominate the Mediterranean?
- What factors led to empire decline?

### Assessment Ideas

4. **Data Analysis**: Graph population/stats over time
